**Session Title:** The Supreme Enjoyer: Understanding the Lord's Proprietorship

**Gita Reference:** Srimad-Bhagavatam, Canto 4, Chapter 5

**Opening Hook:**
Imagine a world where everything belongs to someone else, and you're just a caretaker. This perspective can revolutionize our understanding of ownership and attachment. As Srila Prabhupada says, "The Lord is the proprietor of everything, and one who knows this is not attached to anything." [SB 4.5.6, Purport](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/4/5/6/)

**Core Teaching:**
In the fourth canto of the Srimad-Bhagavatam, we find a profound discussion on the nature of the Supreme Personality of Godhead as the ultimate enjoyer of all sacrifices. In verse 6, it is stated, "The Supreme Personality of Godhead is the ultimate enjoyer of all sacrifices." [SB 4.5.6, Translation](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/4/5/6/) This concept is crucial in understanding our relationship with the Lord and the world around us.

**Frameworks or Levels:**
There are different levels of understanding when it comes to proprietorship. On one hand, we have the materialistic conception, where we think we are the owners of our possessions and achievements. On the other hand, we have the spiritual understanding, where we recognize the Lord as the ultimate proprietor of everything. This shift in perspective is essential in cultivating detachment and devotion.

**Practical Application:**
Take a moment to reflect on your daily life. What are the things you consider yours? Your possessions, your relationships, your accomplishments? Now, imagine that everything belongs to the Lord, and you're just a caretaker. How would this change your attitude towards these things? Would you be more willing to let go of attachments and offer everything to the Lord?

**Micro-practice:**
For the next week, whenever you're about to say "my" or "mine," pause and replace it with "the Lord's." For example, instead of saying "my car," say "the Lord's car." This simple practice can help you develop a sense of detachment and recognition of the Lord's proprietorship.

**Closing Reflection:**
As Srila Prabhupada says, "One who has taken to Kṛṣṇa consciousness, rendering devotional service to the Lord, is the highest among all yogīs." [SB 4.5.33, Purport](https://vedabase.io/en/library/sb/4/5/33/) By recognizing the Lord's proprietorship and cultivating devotion, we can become the highest among all yogis.
