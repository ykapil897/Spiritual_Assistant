**The Complex Nature of Māyāvādīs**

In the realm of spiritual understanding, the Māyāvādīs, or impersonalists, present a paradoxical case. On one hand, they are considered offenders at the lotus feet of Kṛṣṇa, yet on the other hand, they may be counted among the siddhas, or self-realized souls. This seeming contradiction is clarified by the teachings of Srila Prabhupada, who sheds light on the nature of Māyāvādīs and their relationship with Kṛṣṇa.

**The Offense of Māyāvādīs**

As Srila Prabhupada states, "The Māyāvādīs, they are offenders at the lotus feet of Kṛṣṇa." [Source](https://github.com/iskconpress/books/blob/master/ssr/3.txt/) This offense is rooted in their failure to recognize Kṛṣṇa as the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Instead, they view Him as merely a manifestation of the impersonal Brahman. This misconception leads them to neglect the loving relationship between Kṛṣṇa and His devotees, thereby offending His lotus feet.

**The Siddha Status of Māyāvādīs**

Despite this offense, Māyāvādīs may still be considered siddhas, or self-realized souls. As Srila Prabhupada explains, "Even the Māyāvādīs, they are also counted amongst the siddhas, those who have realized the self." [Source](https://github.com/iskconpress/books/blob/master/ssr/1.txt/) This realization of the self, or ātma-realization, is a significant achievement, as it indicates that the Māyāvādīs have transcended the material plane and understood their true nature as spiritual entities.

**The Envy of Siddhas**

However, this realization does not necessarily guarantee a pure devotion to Kṛṣṇa. As Srila Prabhupada warns, "One who has realized the self, he is called siddha. But even amongst the siddhas, there are persons who are envious of Kṛṣṇa." [Source](https://github.com/iskconpress/books/blob/master/tlk/15.txt/) This envy, or asuya, is a subtle yet significant obstacle on the path of spiritual progress. It indicates that even self-realized souls can remain influenced by the material modes of nature, leading them to harbor resentment towards Kṛṣṇa.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, the Māyāvādīs present a complex case, being both offenders at Kṛṣṇa's lotus feet and self-realized souls. This paradox highlights the importance of understanding the nuances of spiritual development. As devotees, we must be cautious not to fall prey to the pitfalls of envy and misconception, even as we strive for self-realization. By recognizing the limitations of Māyāvādī philosophy and cultivating a pure devotion to Kṛṣṇa, we can ensure a genuine and lasting spiritual growth.

**Reflection**

As we reflect on the nature of Māyāvādīs, let us remember the words of Srila Prabhupada: "The Māyāvādīs, they are offenders at the lotus feet of Kṛṣṇa."
